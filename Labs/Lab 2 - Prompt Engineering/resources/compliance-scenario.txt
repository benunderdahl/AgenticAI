COMPLIANCE REVIEW: Meridian Technologies 10-K Filing (FY 2024)

ITEM 1: BUSINESS
Meridian Technologies is a cybersecurity services company with 12,000
employees across 8 countries (US, UK, Germany, Singapore, India, Brazil,
Japan, Australia). Revenue: $3.2B (FY 2024). The Company classifies as
a "large accelerated filer."

The Company's product lines include:
- MeridianShield: Enterprise endpoint protection platform
- MeridianAI: AI-powered threat detection and response
- MeridianCloud: Cloud security posture management
- MeridianGRC: Governance, risk, and compliance automation

Approximately 40% of revenue is generated outside the United States,
primarily in EUR, GBP, and SGD denominated contracts.

ITEM 1A: RISK FACTORS
The Company identifies 14 risk factors, including:
- Cybersecurity breach risk (detailed, 2 pages) — describes potential impact
  of a security incident on the Company's reputation and customer base, with
  specific mention of prior incidents in the industry.
- Key personnel retention (1 paragraph) — states only "we may lose key
  employees to competitors, which could adversely affect our business."
  No specifics on mitigation strategies or current turnover metrics.
- Regulatory compliance across jurisdictions (1 page) — discusses GDPR,
  CCPA, and Singapore PDPA requirements.
- Supply chain concentration (half page) — notes reliance on three
  cloud infrastructure providers.

NOTABLE OMISSIONS:
- NO mention of climate-related risks (despite SEC climate disclosure
  requirements for large accelerated filers taking effect)
- NO mention of AI/ML governance risks despite the MeridianAI product line
  being a significant and growing revenue source ($380M, 12% of total)

ITEM 7: MANAGEMENT'S DISCUSSION AND ANALYSIS
Revenue grew 18% YoY from $2.71B to $3.20B.
Cloud security segment (MeridianCloud) grew 34%.
MeridianAI segment grew 28% to $380M.

Gross margin declined from 62% to 58% due to increased infrastructure costs.
The MD&A states: "Gross margins declined due to infrastructure investments"
but does NOT discuss:
- The margin decline's expected trajectory or whether it is temporary
- Root cause analysis beyond the one-sentence attribution
- Impact of the recently enacted EU Cyber Resilience Act on operations
  and compliance costs (despite the Company operating in the EU)
- Currency translation effects on international revenue (despite 40%
  of revenue being international with significant EUR/GBP exposure)

SG&A increased 22% ($640M to $781M), outpacing revenue growth.
No explanation provided for the SG&A increase rate exceeding revenue growth.

ITEM 9A: INTERNAL CONTROLS OVER FINANCIAL REPORTING
Management asserts effective internal controls over financial reporting.
The auditor's report concurs. No material weaknesses identified.
One significant deficiency noted: "Certain IT general controls over the
MeridianGRC platform require enhancement" — no further detail provided.
